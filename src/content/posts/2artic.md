No Breakthrough in Regulation
Colombia's push for a deep FIFA World Cup run hit a wall against Switzerland in the Round of 16. Played at BC Place in Vancouver, Canada, the match finished goalless after both regulation and extra time, with neither team able to break the deadlock.
Penalties Prove Costly
The contest ultimately came down to a penalty shootout, and Colombia came out on the losing end, falling 4-3. The result, confirmed as a full-time decision on July 7, 2026, closed out Colombia's involvement in this stage of the tournament while Switzerland moved forward.
