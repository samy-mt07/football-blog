Twenty minutes from time, with Spain already up 2-0, Lamine Yamal turned in a brilliant finish that would have put the semifinal completely out of reach. It didn't count.

## What the officials saw

The goal was ruled out for a narrow offside call in the buildup — the kind of tight margin that video review exists for, and the kind that still leaves a stadium unsure what just happened until the graphic goes up on the big screen.

## Why it didn't change anything

Unlike a lot of disallowed-goal controversies, this one didn't decide the match. Spain were already two goals up and in control; the disallowed goal was a footnote, not a turning point. That's worth noting, because most of the loudest VAR arguments online happen when a decision *does* change a result — this one is a reminder that not every reversed goal is a robbery, sometimes it's just a tight call in a game that was already decided.

## The bigger picture

Yamal has been one of the breakout stories of the tournament at just a teenager. A disallowed goal in a semifinal he was already dominating is unlikely to slow the conversation about him heading into a potential final appearance.
