Spain booked their spot in the final on Tuesday. Wednesday decides who joins them, and it's hard to script a bigger semifinal pairing than England against Argentina.

## What's at stake

The winner plays Spain in New Jersey on July 19 for the title. The loser goes home one match short, which is its own kind of brutal given how the two teams' recent histories with each other have gone.

## The storylines

This fixture carries decades of history between these two federations, on and off the pitch, and every meeting gets framed through that lens whether the current squads like it or not. On the pitch, it's a contrast in identity — Argentina's flair-driven attack against an England side that's built its run on defensive discipline and taking its chances when they come.

## Why it might not be close

Argentina have been chasing a third World Cup and a shot at back-to-back titles, giving this squad extra motivation on top of the rivalry itself. England, meanwhile, have quietly put together one of their most balanced tournament runs in years. Neither side has looked fully convincing throughout, which is usually a sign that the semifinal is where someone finally clicks.

Kickoff is Wednesday at Mercedes-Benz Stadium in Atlanta.
