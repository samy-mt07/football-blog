Throw the defensive tactics manual directly into the Atlantic Ocean. France and England just shared a ten-goal masterpiece in Miami in a match that will be talked about for generations. When the dust finally settled at a rocking Hard Rock Stadium on July 18, 2026, the Three Lions walked away with a chaotic 6-4 victory, claiming World Cup bronze in the highest-scoring knockout game of the modern era.

Historically, the World Cup third-place play-off is treated as a glorified exhibition match, but somebody forgot to tell Didier Deschamps and Thomas Tuchel. From the very first whistle, both European heavyweights traded tactical caution for absolute, unadulterated offensive violence. What transpired over 90 minutes in the Florida heat was a glorious, breathless advertisement for football.

## The Miami Meltdown: A First-Half Blitz for the Ages

England started like a team possessed, eager to banish the demons of their semi-final heartbreak. Within the opening ten minutes, Jude Bellingham dismantled the French midfield with a surging run, feeding Bukayo Saka who slotted a cool finish past Mike Maignan. Before Les Bleus could even gather their senses, Harry Kane doubled the lead in the 17th minute, converting a clinical penalty after Marcus Rashford was brought down in the box. At 2-0, it looked like England would stroll to victory.

But France, armed with the most feared attack in world football, reacted with venom. Kylian Mbappé decided to put on a one-man clinic. In a devastating five-minute window, the French captain sliced through England’s right flank twice. First, he curled an unstoppable effort into the top corner of Jordan Pickford’s net, and then he capitalised on a defensive mix-up between John Stones and Declan Rice to tap in the equaliser. Just like that, it was 2-2, and the Miami crowd was witnessing pure theatre.

Just before the referee could blow for halftime, Bukayo Saka struck again. Shifting the ball onto his favored left foot, the Arsenal winger unleashed a deflected effort that looped over Maignan, sending the English contingent into raptures and securing a 3-2 lead at the break.

## Tactical Anarchy in the Second Half

If fans thought the half-time interval would allow both managers to tighten up their defensive lines, they were dead wrong. The second half began at an even more frenetic pace. France needed just four minutes after the restart to level the scoring once more. Bradley Barcola, a half-time substitute, made an instant impact by latching onto an Antoine Griezmann pass and firing a low drive past Pickford’s outstretched arms. 3-3.

With both midfields virtually non-existent, the match transformed into a transition-heavy basketball game. In the 61st minute, Cole Palmer, fresh off the bench, restored England’s lead with a sublime chip over the advancing French keeper. Five minutes later, it was 5-3. Bellingham capped off a Man of the Match performance by rising highest to power home a Phil Foden corner.

Yet, the French refused to die. In the 78th minute, Antoine Griezmann stood over a free-kick 25 yards out. With trademark elegance, the veteran playmaker curled a gorgeous strike over the wall and in off the post. At 5-4, the tension inside Hard Rock Stadium reached a fever pitch. France poured bodies forward in search of an equalizer, leaving massive chasms at the back.

## Ollie Watkins Seals the Historic Win

With France throwing everyone, including goalkeeper Mike Maignan, forward for a late corner, England launched a devastating counter-attack. In the 89th minute, Ollie Watkins, who had replaced Harry Kane late in the half, carried the ball from the halfway line. With composure that defied the madness surrounding him, Watkins bypassed a desperate French defender and rolled the ball into an empty net to make it 6-4.

The final whistle sparked wild celebrations from the English bench. While third place may not have been the ultimate trophy they crossed the Atlantic to win, the sheer manner of this victory over their oldest rivals will go down in folklore. England had conquered France in a double-figure thriller, providing a fittingly wild conclusion to their American adventure.
