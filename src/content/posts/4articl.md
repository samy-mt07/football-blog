Neither side could find a breakthrough at BC Place in Vancouver, and the Round of 16 tie between Switzerland and Colombia went all the way to penalties.
How it went
Switzerland and Colombia played out a scoreless draw through the full ninety minutes of regulation. Extra time came and went without a goal either, leaving the score locked at 0-0. With nothing to separate the two sides on the pitch, the match went to a penalty shootout to determine who would advance.
The number that decided it
Switzerland converted enough of their spot-kicks to win the shootout 4-3, edging past Colombia in the tightest possible margin. The 0-0 scoreline after 120 minutes underlined just how even the contest had been before penalties intervened.
What's next
With the shootout win, Switzerland move on in the FIFA World Cup 2026 knockout stage, while Colombia's run in the tournament comes to an end. The match, played on July 7, 2026, was officially closed out with a full-time result.
