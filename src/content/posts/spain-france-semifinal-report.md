The tournament's best attack met its best defense on Tuesday night in Dallas, and the defense won comfortably.

## How it went

Spain took the lead midway through the first half when a foul inside the box handed them a penalty, converted calmly to make it 1-0. France struggled to generate clean chances all night against a Spanish backline that had barely been broken down all tournament. Early in the second half, a quick give-and-go opened up space in behind, and Spain doubled their lead to make it 2-0 — a scoreline that held for the rest of the match.

## The stat that says it all

Spain came into this match having conceded only once across their previous six games. France, with the tournament's most feared front line, managed almost nothing in front of goal — their most dangerous attackers were kept largely quiet for the full ninety minutes.

## What's next

Spain move on to their first World Cup final since they won the tournament back in 2010. They'll face the winner of Wednesday's second semifinal to decide who lifts the trophy on July 19.
